How to take Good Notes Part I

Learning how to take good notes in an integral part to any endeavor one may under take it in life. The reason is because human beings are inherently forgetful in the modern age. This however, is not entirely our fault. With the internet becoming a more and more critical part of our lives, the amount of information that humans have access to is higher that it has ever been. With all this information coming at us all the time, it becomes next to impossible to remember all the important things that we need to keep in our minds. Therefore, we can lighten the load by taking note of the things that we find important. In this way we can have the page remember what the important date, fact, or name instead of trying to keep in our heads. This in turn will allow us to have more space to feel less stressed and to be more creative. However, before you can develop your own note taking system, you will need to identify which style of note taking works best for you.

The topic of note taking is a very big and there are as many answers as there are people in the world. The only important thing that matters is that the system that you develop works for you. In order to make this topic easier to understand I have taken the liberty of breaking up note taking into three unique styles. These styles are digital, paper, and hybrid. We take a look at each of these in detail, looking at the strengths and weaknesses of each. This should give you a good idea of which note taking style is the best fit for and the situation that requires you to take notes.

A digital note taking system is one that takes place exclusively on a computer. They typically use apps such as Obsidian [personal favorite], Ever Note, or One Note. These systems have their advantages in the fact that you can make notes faster; your notes can be more easily searched; and you can have the notes backed to the cloud with little to no effort. This will make your notes able to be accessed from anywhere. This also make them able to stand the test of time a lot better than more analog methods. The downside to method is going to be the fact that you always need a computer to have access to your notes. If you are accessing them through the cloud then you will need an internet connection. Also you will not retain the information as well compared to when you write them out by hand.

Paper is considered one of the best and easiest ways to take notes. Not only will you remember the things that you take note of longer. You will never lose access to your notes for as long as you have the physical note book. However, there are quite a few downsides to the style of note taking. The first one being that if you have very slow handwriting then you will have a really hard time with this method. There are ways to speed up your handwriting, such as learning some short hand. However, another thing to consider is that your notes can be more easily damaged. Furthermore, if you use water based inks, like I do, then if a page gets wet the all the ink drains from the page. This can be remedied by using archival inks. You can also stick with ball point pens. However, ball points have more friction with the page which can slow down you handwriting and tire your hand a lot faster.

A hybrid note taking system tries to get the best of both worlds. However, everyone has their own idea on how to undertake the system. I personally think that you should take written notes during the lecture and copy them into a computer system as the end of class.
