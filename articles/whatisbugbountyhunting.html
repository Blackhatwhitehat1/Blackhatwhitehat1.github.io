What is Bug Bounty Hunting?

The internet is a very dangerous place. The best way to describe it would be like a wild west. In this wild west there are outlaws called hackers. These hackers are after the most valuable currency known to man. This currency is what is often called data. This data can contain anything from banking information to classified intulectual property. In order to defend this sensitive data companies often enlist a class of hackers called penetration testers. Their job is to find the vulnerabilities before the real hackers do.

We now need to ask ourselves how does bug bounty hunting fits into this bigger picture. First, we need to understand an important cyber security principle. The feild of Cyber-Security is slit into two main categories, red team and blue team. Blue is on the defense, such as configuring firewalls. Red is on the offense. They try to break the security measures put in place by the blue team. This puts red at an advantage. This because while blue needs to find every single vulnerabilities, red needs to only find one to compromise an entire system.

However, I still have not answered what bug bounty hunting is. Bug bounty hunting is a part of red team operation called penetration testing. Penetration testing is where a company hires a hacker to find vulnerabilities that need to be fixed. In the case of bug bount hunting, hackers must hack and report their findings to the company and are paid on the severity of the vulnerability found.

So how does one get into bug bounty hunting? Their are many different routes you could go. However, the best one that I found is a place called Hacker101 which has video courses on how to do bug bounty hunting. It will teach you about internet protocols and potential exploits within them. They also go over how to correctly report your findings. They also have a free CTF to practice yours skills. However, it gets better because they have a sister domain called Hacker1 which gives you access to bug bounty hunting programs. Some of the names that will mention hear are Dyson, Lowes, and many others. However, these are public programs so you need to be carefull not to reoport a bug that has already been reported. I will also mention when a company does this they are often called Vulnerability Disclosure Program. The best part about all this is that it is free. From the training to joining the programs is free but you have to be willing to put the work in because it is far from easy money. So if you want to become a bug bounty hunter. What are you waiting for? You can only make money doing this.
